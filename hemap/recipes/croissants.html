Flaky Croissants

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (cold)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 large egg (beaten)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter (softened for rolling)

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water. Let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Cut in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Add yeast mixture, milk, and beaten egg to the flour mixture. Mix until a soft dough forms.
  4. Cover and refrigerate the dough for at least 1 hour.
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface into a rectangle. Spread softened butter over the dough, then fold it into thirds. Repeat the rolling and folding process three times, refrigerating between each fold.
  6. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  7. Roll out the dough one final time and cut it into triangles. Roll each triangle from the base to the tip to form croissants.
  8. Place the croissants on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes until golden brown and flaky.
